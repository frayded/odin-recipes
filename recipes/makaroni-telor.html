Makaroni Telor Recipe

Makaroni Telor, My Very Go-to When Broke AF

Makaroni Telor ("Macaroni and Egg," the right spelling for egg is "telur" but who cares, really) is a fairly new snack, made of boiled makaroni then fried together with stirred egg, served with seasoning powder of your choice

Very bare minimum and doesn't sound too healthy but it gets the job done! It's filling, cheap, and easy!

And most importantly, it's your playground to do the "cooking with feeling" thing because this recipe doesn't have specific measurement, so in case if you messed up with, say, the amount of salt you put into the stirred egg mixture (happens occasionaly to me), it won't be too messed up for you to eat.

(A little disclaimer, this is my own take on the recipe so... It won't be as good as the ones sold by street food stalls but I'm proud of it and it's still a decent recipe, I promise!)

Ingredients:

  • Macaroni (however much you like)
  • Egg (however many you like, I usually go for two)
  • Seasoning powder (whatever taste you like, I usually go with barbecue-flavored powder)
  • Cooking oil

Steps:

  1. Boil the macaroni till it is cooked. Al dente would do but I often make it softer than that.
  2. Crack and beat 2 eggs (or any amount you like), add a pinch of salt into it.
  3. Heat some cooking oil, just enough to saute the macaroni and enough to fry the egg later so it won't be too oily (as I said before, this is your playground to experiment).
  4. Saute the previously boiled macaroni for 30-60 seconds. After that, pour the beaten egg mixture onto it.
  5. Fry the egg until it is cooked.
  6. Mix macaroni and egg with seasoning powder of your liking. Serve while warm.
