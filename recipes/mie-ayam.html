Mie Ayam

Nothing Like Savory Gravy Soup of Mie Ayam

Another favorite local dish of mine, Mie Ayam or transliterated as "Chicken Noodles." It is derived from Chinese Chicken Noodles brought by Chinese-Indonesians since a long, long time ago (let’s not get too specific with its history, the last time I opened my history book was 3 and a half years ago because it was a mandatory subject).

Made of boiled wheat noodles doused with chicken soup. There are variantas with thicker and thinner soup. Essentially, the soup is all thin, but what makes each soup might have a different texture, from what I have observed at least, is the tiny teensy gravy from chicken topping which comes from one of the most popular variant-by-region, Mie Ayam Solo from Solo (officially Surakarta) in Central Java.

I can’t explain the intricacies of itself and each variant scattered across the country, so please refer to this Wikipedia page for more about Mie Ayam.

Without further ado, let’s get started!

(oh right, another disclaimer: I’ve never made this one myself so… i took it up from an Indonesian page. Click here to see the original recipe post)

Ingredients

Main Ingredients:

  • 3 pieces of dry yellow noodles
  • 2 spring onions
  • Choy sum/caisim (adjustable to your liking, I don’t like veggies in general so I usually omit this)
  • Fried shallots (adjustable to your liking)

Chicken Oil:

  • 200 gr of chicken skin
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • 5 tbsp of cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper powder as needed

Soup:

  • 500 gr of chicken
  • 1 onion
  • 6 pieces of shallots
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 2 cm of galangal
  • 2 cm of ginger
  • 1 cm of turmeric
  • 1 tsp of coriander
  • 4 lime leaves
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 5 tbsp of sweet soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp of sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp of soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp of oyster sauce
  • 1/2 tsp of nutmeg powder
  • salt, sugar, and pepper powder as needed
  • water and cooking oil as needed

Steps:

  1. Boil about 1 liter of water or as needed. Add chicken and cook for 15 minutes or until cooked. Drain and cut the chicken into small pieces. Set aside. Separate the bones to make the soup.
  2. For noodle soup, boil 700 ml of water. Add the chicken bones and boil for about 30 minutes over low heat.
  3. While waiting for the soup to be ready, make the chicken oil. Heat enough oil, add the chicken skin, fry over low heat until cooked and the skin dries up to release oil. Chop the garlic, sauté briefly, turn off the heat. Add salt and pepper powder, mix well.
  4. Chop the onions, shallots, and garlic. Mash the turmeric and coriander, crush the galangal and ginger. Wash the bay leaves and lime leaves.
  5. Heat enough oil, stir-fry all the onion spices until they start to brown. Add a little chicken stock. Add sugar, salt, pepper, nutmeg powder, sweet soy sauce, soy sauce, oyster sauce and sesame oil. Stir well until boiling, then add the chicken. (This will result as the chicken oil as well as the chicken topping that will be used later).
  6. Boil dry noodles until cooked. Drain and pour into a bowl.
  7. Add 2 tablespoons of chicken oil, stir until not sticky. Give a sprinkling of spring onions, fried shallots and choy sum/caisim that have been boiled. Pour in the chicken and soup sufficiently.
