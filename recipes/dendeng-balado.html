Dendeng Balado Recipe

Beef Jerky but... Spicier?

There is this nice local dish I really like. It is called Dendeng Balado

It is a dish local to West Sumatera. Made of thinly sliced beef, fried, and served with Sambal Balado. Don't worry, in most cases, Sambal Balado isn't always that spicy. Take this from someone who can barely handle spiciness than average Indonesian.

Its appearance might reminds you of beef jerky. I don't know, though, never had one.

But yeah, it's really nice. Was thinking to use mom's recipe but I'm kinda lazy to ask so I'll just use this recipe I found from the internet.

(Oh, this recipe is translated from an Indonesian page so... pardon the sloppiness in translation?)

Ingredients

Basic ingredients:

  • 1 kg of beef
  • 2 liters of water
  • 1 tsp white pepper powder
  • 3 cloves of garlic, crushed
  • 3 pieces of star anise
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 400 ml oil, for frying

Marination:

  • 3 shallots
  • 3 cloves of garlic
  • 1 tsp of white pepper powder
  • 1 tsp of coriander, roasted
  • 1 tbsp of tamarind solution
  • half tsp of salt
  • 1 tsp of sugar
  • 400 ml of water

Sambal balado ingredients:

  • 10 large red chilies, roughly chopped
  • 8 shallots
  • 1 tomato
  • 1 tbsp of tamarind solution
  • 1 tsp of salt
  • 300 ml of water
  • 2 tbsp of coconut oil, for for stir-frying

Steps:

  1. For the meat: boil the water, add the meat, pepper, garlic, star anise and salt. Boil the meat until tender.
  2. Drain the meat, thinly slice along with the fibers, beat the meat with a meat mallet.
  3. Mix all the marination ingredients in a bowl, soak the meat for about 1 hour.
  4. Heat the oil, fry the meat until cooked and brown. Lift and let it drain
  5. Making sambal balado: boil the chilies, shallots and tomatoes until soft, remove and drain.
  6. Coarsely grind the lado seasoning with salt and tamarind solution
  7. Heat coconut oil, saute the balado seasoning until fragrant
  8. Mix the meat and sambal balado together, serve while warm (or cool too, it is as good)
